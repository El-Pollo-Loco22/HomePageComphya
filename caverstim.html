Understanding CaverSTIM™

Learn more about how our technology works

Comprehensive Approach to Nerve Support

CaverSTIM™ represents a paradigm shift in how we approach erectile dysfunction following prostate surgery. Rather than treating symptoms after permanent nerve damage has occurred, our system is designed to be placed during the initial surgical procedure.

By delivering targeted neuromodulation during the critical recovery window, CaverSTIM™ aims to support natural nerve function and potentially improve long-term outcomes for patients.

The system consists of three key components: an implantable electrode array placed during surgery, a subcutaneous pulse generator, and a clinician-controlled programming system.

Advanced Neuromodulation Technology

Built on proven neuromodulation principles, CaverSTIM™ uses precisely calibrated electrical stimulation to support nerve activity in the cavernous nerves—the key pathways responsible for erectile function.

The system features:

  • Biocompatible electrode arrays designed for precise nerve contact
  • Rechargeable pulse generator with customizable stimulation parameters
  • Wireless programming for clinician control and adjustment
  • Real-time monitoring capabilities for optimal therapy delivery

Technical Specifications

Electrode Array: Multi-contact design optimized for cavernous nerve engagement

Pulse Generator: Rechargeable lithium-ion battery with wireless charging capability; approximately 6-8 hours per charge cycle for typical stimulation protocols

Stimulation Parameters: Frequency: 5-50 Hz; Pulse width: 100-500 μs; Amplitude: Adjustable based on patient response

Size & Placement: Generator dimensions: 45mm x 35mm x 8mm; Subcutaneous placement in lower abdomen

Compatibility: Designed for use with robotic-assisted and laparoscopic radical prostatectomy procedures

How It Works

A step-by-step look at the CaverSTIM™ treatment pathway

01

Surgical Placement

During radical prostatectomy, the electrode array is carefully positioned adjacent to the cavernous nerve bundles. This is done by the surgeon as part of the standard procedure, requiring no additional invasive steps.

02

System Activation

Following a brief recovery period, the pulse generator is activated by the clinical team. Initial stimulation parameters are set based on patient-specific factors and surgical observations.

03

Ongoing Therapy

The system delivers scheduled neuromodulation therapy over several weeks to months. The stimulation protocol is designed to support nerve signaling during the critical regeneration window.

04

Monitoring & Adjustment

Regular follow-up visits allow clinicians to assess progress and adjust therapy parameters as needed. The wireless programming system enables precise, non-invasive modifications to optimize outcomes.

Clinical Evidence & Development

CaverSTIM™ is currently being evaluated in clinical trials across multiple regions. Our development program is designed to demonstrate both safety and efficacy through rigorous scientific investigation.

  • First-in-human studies completed in Australia with promising early signals
  • Active pilot trial underway at Johns Hopkins University
  • No serious safety concerns reported to date across all study participants
  • FDA Investigational Device Exemption (IDE) approved for U.S. clinical investigation
  • Pivotal trial planned for 2026 to support regulatory approval

CaverSTIM™ is an investigational device not approved for commercial use. Safety and efficacy have not been established.
