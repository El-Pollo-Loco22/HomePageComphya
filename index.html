Challenge: Protecting Nerve Function in Prostate Cancer Surgery

Erectile dysfunction is one of the most common complications after radical prostatectomy — affecting up to 70% of men who undergo the procedure. Even when surgeons use nerve-sparing techniques, the delicate cavernous nerves that control erections can be stretched, overheated, or damaged during surgery.

For most patients, recovery of natural function is slow and incomplete. Current treatments — from pills to pumps — manage symptoms, but none address the underlying nerve injury itself.

CaverSTIM™ aims to change that.

01

Before Surgery – The Diagnosis

Understanding the Challenge

Prostate cancer is one of the most common cancers in men, with over 400,000 prostatectomies performed each year across the U.S. and Europe. For many, the goal is not only survival but preserving quality of life.

02

During Surgery – Nerve Risk

The Critical Moment

Even with nerve-sparing robotic techniques, microscopic trauma to the cavernous nerves is often unavoidable. Studies show 60-70% of men experience erectile dysfunction following surgery, and fewer than 1 in 5 recover baseline function.

03

After Surgery – The Recovery Gap

The Current Reality

Nerve regeneration can take months or years, and for many, it never fully returns. Current options like pills, injections, and pumps focus on symptom management — not nerve healing. The impact goes beyond the physical: over 50% of men with post-surgical ED experience depression or loss of confidence, and 1 in 3 report a diminished sense of identity.

04

The Vision – A New Path Forward

Reimagining Recovery

CaverSTIM™ is being developed to support nerve activity at the critical moment — during surgery — when intervention may have the greatest potential to preserve function and improve long-term outcomes.

CaverSTIM™ is an investigational device not approved for commercial use.
