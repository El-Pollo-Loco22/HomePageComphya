01

Before Surgery – The Diagnosis

Understanding the Challenge

Prostate cancer is one of the most common cancers in men, with over 400,000 prostatectomies performed each year across the U.S. and Europe. For many, the goal beyond survival is quality of life.

02

During Surgery – Nerve Damage Risk

The Critical Moment

Even with nerve-sparing robotic techniques, microscopic trauma to the cavernous nerves is often unavoidable. Studies show 60-70% of men experience erectile dysfunction following surgery, and fewer than 1 in 5 recover baseline function.

03

After Surgery – The Recovery Gap

The Current Reality

Nerve regeneration can take months or years, and for many, it never fully returns. Current options like pills, injections, pumps and prostheses focus on symptom management — not nerve healing. The impact goes beyond the physical: over 50% of men with post-surgical ED experience depression or loss of confidence, and 1 in 3 report a diminished sense of identity.

04

The Vision – A New Path Forward

Reimagining Recovery

CaverSTIM™ is being developed to enable nerve healing from the critical moment - following surgery - when intervention may have the greatest potential to preserve function and improve long-term outcomes.

CaverSTIM™ is an investigational device not approved for commercial use.

Our Approach

Our technology activates the body's own neural pathways, aiming to restore erectile function in a way that feels natural rather than contrived.

How CaverSTIM™ Works

  • Implanted quickly and easily during prostate cancer surgery
  • Electrodes put in place near the pelvic nerves
  • Connected to a slim implantable pulse generator* implanted under the skin in the lower abdomen. A programmed daily neurostimulation routine, below the sensory threshold, supports nerve rehabilitation after surgery for those nerves most at risk from prostatectomy

Supporting Nerve Function at the Source
CaverSTIM™ is an investigational, implantable neuromodulation system designed to support cavernous nerve signaling following surgery, instead of surrounding surgery. By delivering targeted stimulation along the nerve pathways (on the pelvic floor) to the nerves most often affected during radical prostatectomy, CaverSTIM™ aims to enable nerve activity at a time when injury can lead to long-term dysfunction. A generally imperceptible daily stimulation routine begins at the crucial time for nerve recovery.
Implantable Electrode Array

Placed during surgery to align with nerve pathways, no additional procedure needed.

Subcutaneous Pulse Generator

A small, rechargeable unit placed under the skin, programmed by clinicians and tailored to patient needs.

Neuromodulation Protocol

Stimulation begins shortly after surgery, aligning with critical windows for nerve recovery.

Comphya is a Swiss clinical-stage medical device company developing CaverSTIM™, an implantable neuromodulation system intended to support nerve function following radical prostatectomy.
